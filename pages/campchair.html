CAMPCHAIR SEAT

WORK IN PROGRESS

How nice it is to sit around a campfire and listen to the sounds of the natural environment, away from the shrouding of urban population. The only gripe, is finding something to sit on. In the realm of campchairs, you encounter a "pick two" situation, where your options are quality / cost / size. High quality, compact chairs cost a fortune, inexpensive chairs with reasonable durability are too big for backpacking, and smaller inexpensive chairs are poor quality. I discovered this personally, when the seat on my tripod stool started to tear at the seams. I had some high quality canvas from an old project, so I decided to create a new and improved seat.

image here

After I tore out the stitches of the original seat, I was able to reverse engineer a flat pattern with fewer pieces, which would be stronger than the original.

image here

Since stools don't have armrests to mount pockets on, I decided to sew up a small "hammock pocket" underneath the seat.
